# OctoAcme Project Management Overview

OctoAcme runs projects with a clear, stage-gated lifecycle—initiation, planning, execution, release, and retrospective—backed by a small set of standard artifacts (Project One-pager, roadmap/release plan, backlog, risk register, and retrospectives). Initiation validates the problem, aligns stakeholders, and produces a one‑pager capturing objectives, success metrics, and an initial risk list. Planning turns approved initiatives into a prioritized, estimated backlog with a Definition of Done and a milestone map; templates and checklists ensure work is broken into shippable increments and dependencies are identified early.

Day-to-day execution emphasizes lightweight, repeatable workflows: teams use a project board (Backlog → Ready → In Progress → In Review → QA → Done), create small PRs with linked issues and acceptance criteria, and rely on CI gates (tests, linting, and security scans) before review. Estimation and timeboxed planning ensure predictable pulls of DoD-compliant work. Cross-team dependencies are flagged on boards and escalated through a defined blocker path (team → PM → Product Lead → Sponsor).

Roles and personas are explicit to reduce single-person dependencies: Product Managers set vision and success metrics; Project Managers coordinate delivery, schedules, risks, and stakeholder communication; Developers implement and test; QA validates acceptance criteria; stakeholders provide input and approvals. Communication cadence includes daily standups for blockers and progress, weekly delivery syncs for status and risk review, PM+PdM weekly alignment, monthly stakeholder updates, and demos at the end of sprints or milestones.

Quality assurance is integrated into CI and release practices: unit and integration tests, end-to-end smoke tests for critical flows, automated security scanning, and manual QA as needed. PR and CI gating require passing tests and linting and at least one approval before merge. Releases follow a checklist (staging smoke tests, post-deploy verifications, rollback/mitigation plan, release notes) and are followed by retrospectives to capture learnings and action items.
